# § 53.1-153 Eligibility of persons sentenced to jails for more than twelve months.

<p>Persons convicted of felonies or misdemeanors who are sentenced to jails and not eligible for parole under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/53.1-152/'>53.1-152</a>, shall be eligible for parole in the same manner as provided in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/53.1-151/'>53.1-151</a> when the total sentences to be served, exclusive of fines, are more than twelve months. However, a person convicted of misdemeanors and sentenced to serve a total active sentence of more than 12 months in jail shall not be eligible for parole nor subject to the provisions of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/53.1-159/'>53.1-159</a> upon conviction of any offenses committed on or after July 1, 2008.</p><p>The Virginia Parole Board shall have the same powers and duties to carry out the provisions of this section as are set forth in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/53.1-136/'>53.1-136</a>.</p><p>Code 1950, § 53-251.2; 1962, c. 326; 1970, c. 648; 1981, c. 392; 1982, c. 636; 1983, c. 509; 2008, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?081+ful+CHAP0846'>846</a>.</p>
