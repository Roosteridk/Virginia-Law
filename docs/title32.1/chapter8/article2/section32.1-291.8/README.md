# § 32.1-291.8 Preclusive effect of anatomical gift, amendment, or revocation.

<p>A. Except as otherwise provided in subsection G and subject to subsection F, in the absence of an express, contrary indication by the donor, a person other than the donor is barred from making, amending, or revoking an anatomical gift of a donor's body or part if the donor made an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.5/'>32.1-291.5</a> or an amendment to an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.6/'>32.1-291.6</a>.</p><p>B. A donor's revocation of an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.6/'>32.1-291.6</a> is not a refusal and does not bar another person specified in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.4/'>32.1-291.4</a> or <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.9/'>32.1-291.9</a> from making an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.5/'>32.1-291.5</a> or <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.10/'>32.1-291.10</a>.</p><p>C. If a person other than the donor makes an unrevoked anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.5/'>32.1-291.5</a> or an amendment to an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.6/'>32.1-291.6</a>, another person may not make, amend, or revoke the gift of the donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.10/'>32.1-291.10</a>.</p><p>D. A revocation of an anatomical gift of a donor's body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.6/'>32.1-291.6</a> by a person other than the donor does not bar another person from making an anatomical gift of the body or part under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.5/'>32.1-291.5</a> or <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.10/'>32.1-291.10</a>.</p><p>E. In the absence of an express, contrary indication by the donor or other person authorized to make an anatomical gift under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.4/'>32.1-291.4</a>, an anatomical gift of a part is neither a refusal to give another part nor a limitation on the making of an anatomical gift of another part at a later time by the donor or another person.</p><p>F. In the absence of an express, contrary indication by the donor or other person authorized to make an anatomical gift under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.4/'>32.1-291.4</a>, an anatomical gift of a part for one or more of the purposes set forth in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.4/'>32.1-291.4</a> is not a limitation on the making of an anatomical gift of the part for any of the other purposes by the donor or any other person under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.5/'>32.1-291.5</a> or <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/32.1-291.10/'>32.1-291.10</a>.</p><p>G. If a donor who is an unemancipated minor dies, a parent of the donor who is reasonably available may revoke or amend an anatomical gift of the donor's body or part.</p><p>H. If an unemancipated minor who signed a refusal dies, a parent of the minor who is reasonably available may revoke the minor's refusal.</p><p>2007, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?071+ful+CHAP0092'>92</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?071+ful+CHAP0907'>907</a>.</p>
