# ยง 46.2-1709 Business and equipment requirements.

<p>A. A training provider shall:</p><p>1. Permit the Department and FMCSA to conduct random examinations, inspections, and audits of its records, facilities, and operations that relate to the entry-level driver training program without prior notice;</p><p>2. Use vehicles that comply with all federal and state safety requirements and are in the same group and type that the driver trainees intend to operate for the commercial driver's license skills test;</p><p>3. Require all driver trainees to certify that they will comply with state and federal laws and regulations and local laws related to alcohol and controlled substances testing, age requirements for driving commercial vehicles, medical certifications, licensing, and driver records;</p><p>4. Verify that all accepted behind-the-wheel applicants hold a valid commercial learner's permit or commercial driver's license;</p><p>5. Electronically transmit, by midnight of the second business day after the driver trainee completes the training, the driver trainee's certification information through the federal Training Provider Registry website including:</p><p>a. Driver-trainee name, license or permit number, and state of licensure;</p><p>b. Type of class or endorsement training the driver trainee completed;</p><p>c. Total number of clock hours the driver trainee spent to complete the behind-the-wheel training, if applicable;</p><p>d. Name of the training provider and its unique Training Provider Registry number; and</p><p>e. Date or dates of successful training completion.</p><p>6. Update the Entry-Level Driver Training Provider Registration Form once every two years;</p><p>7. Report to FMCSA changes to key information on the Entry-Level Driver Training Provider Registration Form within 30 days of such changes;</p><p>8. Maintain documentation of the school's licensure, registration, certification or authorization to provide training in Virginia;</p><p>9. Ensure that all records specified in ยง <a href='/vacode/46.2-1710/'>46.2-1710</a> are available to FMCSA or its authorized representative, upon request, and provide such records to FMCSA within 48 hours of such request; and</p><p>10. Administer both the range and public road portion of the behind-the-wheel curriculum.</p><p>B. If a training provider receives notice of proposed removal from FMCSA pursuant to 49 C.F.R. ยง 380.723, the training provider shall (i) notify all current driver trainees and driver trainees scheduled for future training of such receipt and (ii) provide a copy of the notice to the Department within one business day of receiving such notice.</p><p>C. If a training provider is removed from the federal Training Provider Registry by FMCSA pursuant to 49 C.F.R. ยง 380.723, such training provider shall (i) cease providing entry-level driver training upon receipt and in accordance with FMCSA guidance and (ii) provide the Department with a copy of the notice of proposed removal within one business day of receipt. No training conducted after the date of removal from the federal Training Provider Registry shall be considered valid.</p><p>2019, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?191+ful+CHAP0750'>750</a>; 2020, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?201+ful+CHAP0546'>546</a>.</p>
