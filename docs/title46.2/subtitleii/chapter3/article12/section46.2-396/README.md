# § 46.2-396 Suspension of license for reckless driving resulting in death of any person.

<p>When any person is convicted of reckless driving as provided for in §§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-853/'>46.2-853</a> through <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-864/'>46.2-864</a> and the reckless driving was the cause of the death of any person, then in addition to any other penalties provided by law, the driver's license of the person may be suspended by the court for no more than twelve months. In case of conviction the court may order the surrender of the license to the court where it shall be disposed of in accordance with the provisions of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-398/'>46.2-398</a>. If the person so convicted has not obtained a license required by this chapter or is a nonresident, the court may direct in the judgment of conviction that the person shall not drive any motor vehicle in the Commonwealth for a period not to exceed twelve months. The fact of the suspension shall not be admissible as evidence in any related civil proceeding.</p><p>1976, c. 320, § 46.1-423.4; 1984, c. 780; 1989, c. 727.</p>
