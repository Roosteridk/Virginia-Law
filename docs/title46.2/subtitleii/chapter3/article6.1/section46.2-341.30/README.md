# § 46.2-341.30 Disqualification for driving commercial motor vehicle while intoxicated, etc.

<p>A. The judgment of conviction under any provision of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-341.24/'>46.2-341.24</a> shall of itself operate to disqualify the person so convicted from the privilege to drive or operate any commercial motor vehicle as provided in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-341.18/'>46.2-341.18</a>. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, such disqualification shall not be subject to any suspension, reduction, limitation or other modification by the court or the Commissioner.</p><p>B. A judgment of conviction under any provision of subsection A of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/46.2-341.24/'>46.2-341.24</a>, in addition to causing the disqualification under subsection A of this section, shall also operate to deprive the person so convicted of his privilege to drive or operate any motor vehicle as provided in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/18.2-271/'>18.2-271</a>.</p><p>1989, c. 705, § 46.1-372.29.</p>
