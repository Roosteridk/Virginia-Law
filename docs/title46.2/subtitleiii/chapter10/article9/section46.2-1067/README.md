# ยง 46.2-1067 Within what distances brakes should stop vehicle.

<p>On a dry, hard, approximately level stretch of highway free from loose material, the service braking system shall be capable of stopping a motor vehicle or combination of vehicles at all times and under all conditions of loading at a speed of 20 miles per hour within the following distances:</p><p>1. Passenger motor vehicles, except buses and antique vehicles, 25 feet.</p><p>2. Buses, trucks, and tractor trucks, 40 feet.</p><p>3. Motor vehicles registered or qualified to be registered as antique vehicles, when equipped with two-wheel brakes, 45 feet; four-wheel brakes, 25 feet.</p><p>4. All combinations of vehicles, 40 feet.</p><p>5. Motorcycles or autocycles, 30 feet.</p><p>Code 1950, ยง 46-284; 1958, c. 541, ยง 46.1-278; 1968, c. 164; 1970, c. 28; 1972, c. 3; 1989, c. 727; 2014, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?141+ful+CHAP0053'>53</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?141+ful+CHAP0256'>256</a>.</p>
