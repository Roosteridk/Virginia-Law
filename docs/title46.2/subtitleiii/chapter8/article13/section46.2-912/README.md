# ยง 46.2-912 Operating motorcycle without headlight, horn or rearview mirror.

<p>A. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, motorcycles may be operated without headlights, horns, or rearview mirrors on public highways if all the following conditions are met:</p><p>1. The motorcycles are designed for use in trail riding and endurance runs;</p><p>2. The motorcycles are being driven by duly licensed persons;</p><p>3. The motorcycles are being operated between sunrise and sunset; and</p><p>4. The motorcycles are being operated during endurance runs sanctioned by the American Motorcycle Association.</p><p>B. No person shall operate motorcycles without such equipment on the public highways of the Commonwealth other than at the times and under the circumstances set forth in this section.</p><p>1970, c. 300, ยง 46.1-172.01; 1978, c. 605; 1989, c. 727.</p>
