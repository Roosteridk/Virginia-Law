# ยง 64.2-2722 Disposition of unappointed property under released or unexercised general power.

<p>To the extent that a powerholder releases or fails to exercise a general power of appointment other than a power to withdraw property from, revoke, or amend a trust:</p><p>1. The gift-in-default clause controls the disposition of the unappointed property; or</p><p>2. If there is no gift-in-default clause or to the extent that the clause is ineffective:</p><p>a. Except as otherwise provided in subdivision 2 b, the unappointed property passes to:</p><p>(1) The powerholder if the powerholder is a permissible appointee and living; or</p><p>(2) If the powerholder is an impermissible appointee or deceased, the powerholder's estate if the estate is a permissible appointee; or</p><p>b. To the extent that the powerholder released the power, or if there is no taker under subdivision 2 a, the unappointed property passes under a reversionary interest to the donor or the donor's transferee or successor in interest.</p><p>2016, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?161+ful+CHAP0266'>266</a>.</p>
