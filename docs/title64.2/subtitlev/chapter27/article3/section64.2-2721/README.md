# ยง 64.2-2721 Capture doctrine; disposition of ineffectively appointed property under general power.

<p>To the extent that a powerholder of a general power of appointment, other than a power to withdraw property from, revoke, or amend a trust, makes an ineffective appointment:</p><p>1. The gift-in-default clause controls the disposition of the ineffectively appointed property; or</p><p>2. If there is no gift-in-default clause or to the extent that the clause is ineffective, the ineffectively appointed property:</p><p>a. Passes to:</p><p>(1) The powerholder if the powerholder is a permissible appointee and living; or</p><p>(2) If the powerholder is an impermissible appointee or deceased, the powerholder's estate if the estate is a permissible appointee; or</p><p>b. If there is no taker under subdivision 2 a, passes under a reversionary interest to the donor or the donor's transferee or successor in interest.</p><p>2016, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?161+ful+CHAP0266'>266</a>.</p>
