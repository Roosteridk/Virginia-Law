# ยง 64.2-2717 Permissible appointment.

<p>A. A powerholder of a general power of appointment that permits appointment to the powerholder or the powerholder's estate may make any appointment, including an appointment in trust or creating a new power of appointment, that the powerholder could make in disposing of the powerholder's own property.</p><p>B. A powerholder of a general power of appointment that permits appointment only to the creditors of the powerholder or of the powerholder's estate may appoint only to those creditors.</p><p>C. Unless the terms of the instrument creating a power of appointment manifest a contrary intent, the powerholder of a nongeneral power may:</p><p>1. Make an appointment in any form, including an appointment in trust, in favor of a permissible appointee;</p><p>2. Create a general power or a nongeneral power in a permissible appointee; or</p><p>3. Create a nongeneral power in an impermissible appointee to appoint to one or more of the permissible appointees of the original nongeneral power.</p><p>2016, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?161+ful+CHAP0266'>266</a>.</p>
