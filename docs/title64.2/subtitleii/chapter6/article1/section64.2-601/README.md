# § 64.2-601 Payment or delivery of small asset by affidavit.

<p>A. Any person having possession of a small asset shall pay or deliver the small asset to the designated successor of the decedent upon being presented an affidavit made by all of the known successors stating:</p><p>1. That the value of the decedent's entire personal probate estate as of the date of the decedent's death, wherever located, does not exceed $50,000;</p><p>2. That at least 60 days have elapsed since the decedent's death;</p><p>3. That no application for the appointment of a personal representative is pending or has been granted in any jurisdiction;</p><p>4. That the decedent's will, if any, was duly probated;</p><p>5. That the claiming successor is entitled to payment or delivery of the small asset, and the basis upon which such entitlement is claimed;</p><p>6. The names and addresses of all successors, to the extent known;</p><p>7. The name of each successor designated to receive payment or delivery of the small asset on behalf of all successors; and</p><p>8. That the designated successor shall have a fiduciary duty to safeguard and promptly pay or deliver the small asset as required by the laws of the Commonwealth.</p><p>B. The designated successor may discharge his fiduciary duty to promptly pay or deliver the small asset to a successor who is, or is reasonably believed to be, incapacitated or under a legal disability, by paying or delivering the asset directly to the incapacitated or disabled successor or applying it for such successor's benefit, or by:</p><p>1. Paying it to such successor's conservator or, if no conservator exists, guardian;</p><p>2. Paying it to such successor's custodian under the Virginia Uniform Transfers to Minors Act (§ <a href='/vacode/64.2-1900/'>64.2-1900</a> et seq.) or custodial trustee under the Uniform Custodial Trust Act (§ <a href='/vacode/64.2-900/'>64.2-900</a> et seq.), and, for that purpose, creating a custodianship or custodial trust;</p><p>3. If the designated successor does not know of a conservator, guardian, custodian, or custodial trustee, paying it to an adult relative or other person having legal or physical care or custody of such successor to be expended on such successor's behalf; or</p><p>4. Managing it as a separate fund on such successor's behalf, subject to such successor's continuing right to withdraw the asset.</p><p>C. Any successor may be represented and bound under virtual representation provisions of §§ <a href='/vacode/64.2-714/'>64.2-714</a>, <a href='/vacode/64.2-716/'>64.2-716</a>, and <a href='/vacode/64.2-717/'>64.2-717</a> with respect to affidavits required and designations of persons to receive payment or delivery of a small asset under this article.</p><p>D. A transfer agent of any security, upon the surrender of the certificates, if any, evidencing the security, shall change the registered ownership on the books of a corporation from the decedent to the designated successor upon the presentation of an affidavit as provided in subsection A.</p><p>E. Upon the presentation of an affidavit as provided in subsection A, the designated successor may endorse or negotiate any small asset that is a check, draft, or other negotiable instrument that is payable to the decedent or the decedent's estate. Notwithstanding the provisions of §§ <a href='/vacode/8.3A-403/'>8.3A-403</a>, <a href='/vacode/8.3A-417/'>8.3A-417</a>, and <a href='/vacode/8.3A-420/'>8.3A-420</a>, a financial institution accepting such check, draft, or other negotiable instrument presented for deposit in such manner is discharged from all claims for the amount accepted.</p><p>1981, c. 281, § 64.1-132.2; 1996, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?961+ful+CHAP0549'>549</a>; 2001, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?011+ful+CHAP0368'>368</a>; 2006, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?061+ful+CHAP0280'>280</a>; 2010, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?101+ful+CHAP0269'>269</a>; 2012, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?121+ful+CHAP0614'>614</a>; 2013, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?131+ful+CHAP0068'>68</a>; 2015, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?151+ful+CHAP0617'>617</a>; 2019, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?191+ful+CHAP0360'>360</a>.</p>
