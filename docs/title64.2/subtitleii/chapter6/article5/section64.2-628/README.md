# § 64.2-628 Requirements.

<p>A transfer on death deed:</p><p>1. Except as otherwise provided in subdivision 2, shall contain the essential elements and formalities of a properly recordable inter vivos deed or document to convey a cooperative interest created pursuant to the Virginia Real Estate Cooperative Act (§ <a href='/vacode/55.1-2100/'>55.1-2100</a> et seq.);</p><p>2. Shall state that the transfer to the designated beneficiary is to occur at the transferor's death;</p><p>3. Shall be recorded before the transferor's death in the land records of the clerk's office of the circuit court in the jurisdiction where the property is located;</p><p>4. Shall comply with the requirements for recordation set forth in Chapter 6 (§ <a href='/vacode/55.1-600/'>55.1-600</a> et seq.) of Title 55.1 and shall be indexed by the clerk of court under the name of the transferor as grantor;</p><p>5. Unless the transfer is for consideration, shall be exempt from recordation tax as provided by subsection J of § <a href='/vacode/58.1-811/'>58.1-811</a>;</p><p>6. For property owned by joint owners to be effective, shall be executed by all joint owners; and</p><p>7. Shall be considered a deed for purposes of complying with the requirements of § <a href='/vacode/17.1-223/'>17.1-223</a>.</p><p>2013, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?131+ful+CHAP0390'>390</a>; 2022, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?221+ful+CHAP0309'>309</a>.</p>
