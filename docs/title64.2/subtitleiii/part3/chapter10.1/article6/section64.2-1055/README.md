# § 64.2-1055 Insubstantial allocation not required.

<p>A. If a fiduciary determines that an allocation between income and principal required by § <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1056/'>64.2-1056</a>, <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1057/'>64.2-1057</a>, <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1058/'>64.2-1058</a>, <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1059/'>64.2-1059</a>, or <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1062/'>64.2-1062</a> is insubstantial, the fiduciary may allocate the entire amount to principal, unless subsection E of § <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1038/'>64.2-1038</a> applies to the allocation.</p><p>B. A fiduciary may presume an allocation is insubstantial under subsection A if:</p><p>1. The amount of the allocation would increase or decrease net income in an accounting period, as determined before the allocation, by less than 10 percent; and</p><p>2. The asset producing the receipt to be allocated has a fair market value less than 10 percent of the total fair market value of the assets owned or held by the fiduciary at the beginning of the accounting period.</p><p>C. The power to make a determination under subsection A may be:</p><p>1. Exercised by a co-fiduciary in the manner described in subsection F of § <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1038/'>64.2-1038</a>; or</p><p>2. Released or delegated for a reason described in subsection G of § <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1038/'>64.2-1038</a> and in the manner described in subsection H of § <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1038/'>64.2-1038</a>.</p><p>2022, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?221+ful+CHAP0354'>354</a>.</p>
