# § 64.2-1069 Income taxes.

<p>A. A tax required to be paid by a fiduciary that is based on receipts allocated to income must be paid from income.</p><p>B. A tax required to be paid by a fiduciary that is based on receipts allocated to principal must be paid from principal, even if the tax is called an income tax by the taxing authority.</p><p>C. Subject to subsection D and §§ <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1067/'>64.2-1067</a>, <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-10687/'>64.2-1068</a>, and <a href='https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title64.2/chapter10.1/section64.2-1070/'>64.2-1070</a>, a tax required to be paid by a fiduciary on a share of an entity's taxable income in an accounting period must be paid from:</p><p>1. Income and principal proportionately to the allocation between income and principal of receipts from the entity in the period; and</p><p>2. Principal, to the extent the tax exceeds the receipts from the entity in the period.</p><p>D. After applying subsections A, B, and C, a fiduciary shall adjust income or principal receipts, to the extent the taxes the fiduciary pays are reduced because of a deduction for a payment made to a beneficiary.</p><p>2022, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?221+ful+CHAP0354'>354</a>.</p>
