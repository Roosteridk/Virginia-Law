# ยง 3.2-5301 Definitions.

<p>As used in this chapter, unless the context requires a different meaning:</p><p>"Consumer" means any person who acquires eggs for consumption in his own household and not for resale.</p><p>"Eggs" mean eggs in the shell that are the product of domesticated chickens.</p><p>"Grade" means specifications defining the limits of variation in quality of eggs in such a manner as to differentiate among classes of eggs, and the letter, number, or other symbol by which references may be made.</p><p>"Institutional consumer" means a restaurant, hotel, boardinghouse, or any other business, facility, or place in which eggs are prepared or offered as food to patrons, residents, inmates, or patients.</p><p>"Market" means sell, offer for sale, give in the channels of commerce, barter, exchange, or distribute in any manner.</p><p>"Processor" includes any person who cleans, candles, grades, sizes, and packs shell eggs for human consumption.</p><p>"Retailer" means any person who markets eggs to consumers.</p><p>"Standard" means specifications of the physical characteristics or any or all of the component parts of individual eggs.</p><p>1968, c. 142, ยง 3.1-763.15; 1972, c. 120; 2008, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?081+ful+CHAP0860'>860</a>.</p>
