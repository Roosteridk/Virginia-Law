# § 54.1-3320 Acts restricted to pharmacists.

<p>A. Within the practice of pharmacy as defined in § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/54.1-3300/'>54.1-3300</a>, the following acts shall be performed by pharmacists, except as provided in subsection B:</p><p>1. The review of a prescription, in conformance with this chapter and Chapter 34 (§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/54.1-3400/'>54.1-3400</a> et seq.) of this title and with current practices in pharmacy, for its completeness, validity, safety, and drug-therapy appropriateness, including, but not limited to, interactions, contraindications, adverse effects, incorrect dosage or duration of treatment, clinical misuse or abuse, and noncompliance and duplication of therapy;</p><p>2. The receipt of an oral prescription from a practitioner or his authorized agent;</p><p>3. The conduct of a prospective drug review and counseling as required by § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/54.1-3319/'>54.1-3319</a> prior to the dispensing or refilling of any prescription;</p><p>4. The provision of information to the public or to a practitioner concerning the therapeutic value and use of drugs in the treatment and prevention of disease;</p><p>5. The communication with the prescriber, or the prescriber's agent, involving any modification other than refill authorization of a prescription or of any drug therapy, resolution of any drug therapy problem, or the substitution of any drug prescribed;</p><p>6. The verification of the accuracy of a completed prescription prior to dispensing the prescription;</p><p>7. The supervision of pharmacy interns and pharmacy technicians; and</p><p>8. Any other activity required by regulation to be performed by a pharmacist.</p><p>B. A pharmacy intern may engage in the acts to be performed by a pharmacist as set forth in subsection A or the Drug Control Act (§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/54.1-3400/'>54.1-3400</a> et seq.) for the purpose of obtaining practical experience required for licensure as a pharmacist, if the supervising pharmacist is directly monitoring these activities.</p><p>C. A registered pharmacy technician, working under the direct supervision of a qualified nuclear pharmacist, as defined by regulations of the Board, may accept oral prescriptions for diagnostic, nonpatient specific radiopharmaceuticals in accordance with subsection C of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/54.1-3410.1/'>54.1-3410.1</a>.</p><p>D. Consistent with patient safety, a pharmacist shall exercise sole authority in determining the maximum number of pharmacy technicians that he shall supervise; however, no pharmacist shall supervise more pharmacy technicians than allowed by Board regulations.</p><p>2001, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?011+ful+CHAP0317'>317</a>; 2005, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?051+ful+CHAP0403'>403</a>; 2006, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?061+ful+CHAP0626'>626</a>; 2010, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?101+ful+CHAP0090'>90</a>.</p>
