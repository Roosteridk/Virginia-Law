# § 55.1-2179 Revocation of registration.

<p>A. The Common Interest Community Board, after providing notice stating the deficiency complained of and holding a hearing, may issue an order revoking the registration of a cooperative upon determination that a declarant or any officer or principal of a declarant has:</p><p>1. Failed to comply with a cease and desist order issued by the Common Interest Community Board affecting that cooperative;</p><p>2. Concealed, diverted, or disposed of any funds or assets of any person in a manner impairing rights of purchasers of cooperative interests in that cooperative;</p><p>3. Failed to perform any stipulation or agreement made to induce the Common Interest Community Board to issue an order relating to that cooperative;</p><p>4. Intentionally misrepresented or failed to disclose a material fact in the application for registration; or</p><p>5. Failed to meet any of the conditions described in §§ <a href='/vacode/55.1-2176/'>55.1-2176</a> and <a href='/vacode/55.1-2177/'>55.1-2177</a> necessary to qualify for registration.</p><p>B. Without the consent of the Common Interest Community Board, a declarant shall not convey, cause to be conveyed, or contract for the conveyance of any cooperative interest while an order revoking the registration of the cooperative is in effect.</p><p>C. In appropriate cases, the Common Interest Community Board may issue a cease and desist order in lieu of an order of revocation.</p><p>1982, c. 277, § 55-501; 2019, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?191+ful+CHAP0712'>712</a>.</p>
