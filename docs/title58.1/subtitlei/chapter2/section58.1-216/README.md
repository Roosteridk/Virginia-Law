# § 58.1-216 Writs, notices, processes, and orders.

<p>A. The Tax Commissioner may, in all matters within his jurisdiction, award and issue and have served, executed and returned any writ, notice, process, order or order of publication which may by law be awarded, issued, served, executed or returned by or to any court in this Commonwealth for the purpose of compelling the attendance of witnesses, the production of books and papers and the enforcement and execution of his findings, orders and judgments. Such a summons to any witness or to produce any document may be personally served by an employee of the Department or served in the manner provided by § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-217/'>58.1-217</a>. But all memoranda of liens for the collection of taxes shall issue under the provisions of § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-1805/'>58.1-1805</a> or § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-1806/'>58.1-1806</a>.</p><p>B. Any person summoned as a witness, or summoned to produce books and papers, or both, who fails or refuses to attend, or to produce such books and papers, or both, may be proceeded against in the circuit court of the city or of the county in which such person resides by a rule or attachment issued on motion of the Tax Commissioner in the name of the Commonwealth to compel such person to attend as a witness, or to produce such books and papers, or both, at such time and place as may be designated by the court.</p><p>C. The Tax Commissioner and such other officers or employees of the Department as the Tax Commissioner may authorize in writing may administer oaths in the performance of their duties.</p><p>Code 1950, §§ 58-35, 58-36, 58-426; 1952, c. 414; 1960, c. 508; 1984, c. 675; 1992, c. 763.</p>
