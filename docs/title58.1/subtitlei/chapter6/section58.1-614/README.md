# § 58.1-614 (Contingent expiration date -- see note*) Vending machine sales.

<p>A. Notwithstanding the provisions of §§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-603/'>58.1-603</a> and <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-604/'>58.1-604</a>, whenever a dealer makes sales of tangible personal property through vending machines, or in any other manner making collection of the tax impractical, as determined by the Tax Commissioner, such dealer shall be required to report his wholesale purchases for sale at retail from vending machines and shall be required to remit an amount based on 5.3 percent of such wholesale purchases. However, any dealer located in any county or city for which the taxes under §§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-603.1/'>58.1-603.1</a> and <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-604.01/'>58.1-604.01</a> are imposed shall be required to remit an amount based on 6.0 percent of such wholesale purchases.</p><p>B. Notwithstanding the provisions of §§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-605/'>58.1-605</a> and <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-606/'>58.1-606</a>, dealers making sales of tangible personal property through vending machines shall report and remit the one percent local sales and use tax computed as provided in subsection A.</p><p>C. The provisions of subsections A and B shall not be applicable to vending machine operators all of whose machines are under contract to nonprofit organizations. Such operators shall report only the gross receipts from machines selling items for more than 10 cents and shall be required to remit an amount based on a percentage of their remaining gross sales established by the Tax Commissioner to take into account the inclusion of sales tax.</p><p>D. Notwithstanding any other provisions in this section, when the Tax Commissioner determines that it is impractical to collect the tax in the manner provided by those sections, such dealer shall be required to remit an amount based on a percentage of gross receipts which takes into account the inclusion of the sales tax.</p><p>E. The provisions of this section shall not be applicable to any dealer who fails to maintain records satisfactory to the Tax Commissioner. A dealer making sales of tangible personal property through vending machines shall obtain a certificate of registration under § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/58.1-613/'>58.1-613</a> in relevant form for each county or city in which he has machines.</p><p>Code 1950, § 58-441.34; 1966, c. 151; 1974, c. 389; 1980, c. 755; 1982, c. 219; 1984, c. 675; 1986, Sp.
