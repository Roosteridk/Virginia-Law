# ยง 58.1-4121 Suspension or revocation of service permit; civil penalty.

<p>A. The Director may suspend, revoke, refuse to renew, or assess a civil penalty against the holder of a service permit in a sum not to exceed $10,000, after notice and a hearing. Such service permit may, however, be temporarily suspended by the Director without prior notice, pending any prosecution, hearing, or investigation, whether by a third party or by the Director. A service permit may be suspended, revoked, or refused renewal by the Director for one or more of the following reasons:</p><p>1. Failure to comply with, or violation of, any provision of this chapter or any regulation or condition of the Department;</p><p>2. Failure to disclose facts during the application process that indicate that such service permit should not have been issued;</p><p>3. Conviction of a felony under the laws of the Commonwealth or any other state or of the United States subsequent to issuance of a service permit;</p><p>4. Failure to file any return or report, keep any record, or pay any fees or other charges required by this chapter;</p><p>5. Any act of fraud, deceit, misrepresentation, or conduct prejudicial to public confidence in the integrity of gaming operations;</p><p>6. A material change, since issuance of the service permit, with respect to any matters required to be considered by the Director under this chapter; or</p><p>7. Other factors established by Department regulation.</p><p>B. Actions taken by the Director pursuant to this section shall be final unless appealed in accordance with ยง <a href='/vacode/58.1-4105/'>58.1-4105</a>. Suspension or revocation of a service permit for any violation shall not preclude criminal liability for such violation.</p><p>2020, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?201+ful+CHAP1197'>1197</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?201+ful+CHAP1248'>1248</a>.</p>
