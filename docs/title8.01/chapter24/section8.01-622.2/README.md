# § 8.01-622.2 Injunction against disclosure of criminal investigative file materials under the Virginia Freedom of Information Act.

<p>Except with respect to the disclosure of records under clause (iv) of subsection D or clause (v) (a) of subsection D of § <a href='/vacode/2.2-3706.1/'>2.2-3706.1</a>, an injunction may be awarded to prevent the disclosure of records related to criminal investigative files requested under § <a href='/vacode/2.2-3706.1/'>2.2-3706.1</a> of the Virginia Freedom of Information Act (§ <a href='/vacode/2.2-3700/'>2.2-3700</a> et seq.).</p><p>In making its determination, a court shall consider the following and shall enter its findings on the record:</p><p>1. Whether disclosure of the public records would constitute an unreasonable invasion of personal privacy;</p><p>2. Whether disclosure of the public records would endanger the life or physical safety of any individual;</p><p>3. Whether disclosure of the public records would subject (i) the victim; (ii) the victim's immediate family members as set forth in § <a href='/vacode/2.2-3706.1/'>2.2-3706.1</a>, if the victim is deceased and the immediate family member is not a person of interest or a suspect in the criminal investigation or proceeding; or (iii) the parent or guardian of the victim, if the victim is a minor and the parent or guardian is not a person of interest or a suspect in the criminal investigation or proceeding, to severe mental or emotional distress; and</p><p>4. Any other factor or information deemed by the court to be relevant.</p><p>2022, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?221+ful+CHAP0386'>386</a>.</p>
