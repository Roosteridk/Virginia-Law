# ยง 8.01-277.1 Objections to personal jurisdiction or defective process; what constitutes waiver.

<p>A. Except as provided in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-277/'>8.01-277</a>, a person waives any objection to personal jurisdiction or defective process if he engages in conduct related to adjudicating the merits of the case, including, but not limited to:</p><p>1. Filing a demurrer, plea in bar, answer, counterclaim, cross-claim, or third-party claim;</p><p>2. Conducting discovery, except as provided in subsection B;</p><p>3. Seeking a ruling on the merits of the case; or</p><p>4. Actively participating in proceedings related to determining the merits of the case.</p><p>B. A person does not waive any objection to personal jurisdiction or defective process if he engages in conduct unrelated to adjudicating the merits of the case, including, but not limited to:</p><p>1. Requesting or agreeing to an extension of time;</p><p>2. Agreeing to a scheduling order;</p><p>3. Conducting discovery authorized by the court related to adjudicating the objection;</p><p>4. Observing or attending proceedings in the case;</p><p>5. Filing a motion to transfer venue pursuant to ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-264/'>8.01-264</a> when such motion is filed contemporaneously with the objection; or</p><p>6. Removing the case to federal court.</p><p>2011, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?111+ful+CHAP0710'>710</a>.</p>
