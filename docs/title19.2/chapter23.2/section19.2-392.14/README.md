# § 19.2-392.14 (For contingent effective date see Acts 2021, Sp. Sess. I, cc. 524 and 542) Disclosure of sealed records; penalty.

<p>A. It is unlawful for any person having or acquiring access to sealed criminal history record information or a court record, including any records relating to an arrest, charge, or conviction, that was ordered to be sealed pursuant to § <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.7/'>19.2-392.7</a>, <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.8/'>19.2-392.8</a>, <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.9/'>19.2-392.9</a>, <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.11/'>19.2-392.11</a>, or <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.12/'>19.2-392.12</a>, to disclose such record or any information from such record to another person, except in accordance with the purposes set forth in § <a href='/vacode/19.2-392.13/'>19.2-392.13</a> and pursuant to the rules and regulations adopted pursuant to § <a href='/vacode/9.1-128/'>9.1-128</a> and the procedures adopted pursuant to § <a href='/vacode/9.1-134/'>9.1-134</a>.</p><p>B. A clerk of court shall not be in violation of this section if such clerk informs a person requesting access to a sealed court record that such court record has been sealed and can only be accessed pursuant to a court order.</p><p>C. Any person who willfully violates this section is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor. Any person who maliciously and intentionally violates this section is guilty of a Class 6 felony.</p><p>2021, Sp. Sess. I, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?212+ful+CHAP0524'>524</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?212+ful+CHAP0542'>542</a>.</p>
