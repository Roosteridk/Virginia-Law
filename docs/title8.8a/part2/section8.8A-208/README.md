# ยง 8.8A-208 Effect of signature of authenticating trustee, registrar or transfer agent.

<p>(a) A person signing a security certificate as authenticating trustee, registrar, transfer agent, or the like, warrants to a purchaser for value of the certificated security, if the purchaser is without notice of a particular defect, that:</p><p>(1) the certificate is genuine;</p><p>(2) the person's own participation in the issue of the security is within the person's capacity and within the scope of the authority received by the person from the issuer; and</p><p>(3) the person has reasonable grounds to believe that the certificated security is in the form and within the amount the issuer is authorized to issue.</p><p>(b) Unless otherwise agreed, a person signing under subsection (a) does not assume responsibility for the validity of the security in other respects.</p><p>1996, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?961+ful+CHAP0216'>216</a>.</p>
