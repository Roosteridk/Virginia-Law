# ยง 59.1-501.6 Rule of construction.

<p>(a) This chapter must be liberally construed and applied to promote its underlying purposes and policies to:</p><p>(1) support and facilitate the realization of the full potential of computer information transactions;</p><p>(2) clarify the law governing computer information transactions;</p><p>(3) enable expanding commercial practice in computer information transactions by commercial usage and agreement of the parties;</p><p>(4) promote uniformity of the law with respect to the subject matter of this chapter among States that enact it; and</p><p>(5) permit the continued expansion of commercial practices in the excluded transactions through custom, usage and agreement of the parties.</p><p>(b) Except as otherwise provided in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/59.1-501.15/'>59.1-501.15</a>, the use of mandatory language or the absence of a phrase such as "unless otherwise agreed" in a provision of this chapter does not preclude the parties from varying the effect of the provision by agreement.</p><p>(c) The fact that a provision of this chapter imposes a condition for a result does not by itself mean that the absence of that condition yields a different result.</p><p>(d) To be enforceable, a term need not be conspicuous, negotiated, or expressly assented or agreed to, unless this chapter expressly so requires.</p><p>2000, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0101'>101</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0996'>996</a>; 2004, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?041+ful+CHAP0794'>794</a>.</p>
