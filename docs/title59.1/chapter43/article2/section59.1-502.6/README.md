# ยง 59.1-502.6 Offer and acceptance; electronic agents.

<p>(a) A contract may be formed by the interaction of electronic agents. If the interaction results in the electronic agents' engaging in operations that under the circumstances indicate acceptance of an offer, a contract is formed, but a court may grant appropriate relief if the operations resulted from fraud, electronic mistake, or the like.</p><p>(b) A contract may be formed by the interaction of an electronic agent and an individual acting on the individual's own behalf or for another person. A contract is formed if the individual takes an action or makes a statement that the individual can refuse to take or say and that the individual has reason to know will:</p><p>(1) cause the electronic agent to perform, provide benefits, or allow the use or access that is the subject of the contract, or send instructions to do so; or</p><p>(2) indicate acceptance, regardless of other expressions or actions by the individual to which the individual has reason to know the electronic agent cannot react.</p><p>(c) The terms of a contract formed under subsection (b) are determined under ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/59.1-502.8/'>59.1-502.8</a> or ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/59.1-502.9/'>59.1-502.9</a> but do not include a term provided by the individual if the individual had reason to know that the electronic agent could not react to the term.</p><p>2000, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0101'>101</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0996'>996</a>.</p>
