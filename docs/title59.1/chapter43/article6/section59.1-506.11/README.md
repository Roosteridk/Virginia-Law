# ยง 59.1-506.11 Access contracts.

<p>(a) If an access contract provides for access over a period of time, the following rules apply:</p><p>(1) The licensee's rights of access are to the information as modified and made commercially available by the licensor from time to time during that period.</p><p>(2) A change in the content of the information is a breach of contract only if the change conflicts with an express term of the agreement.</p><p>(3) Unless it is subject to a contractual use term, information obtained by the licensee is free of any use restriction other than a restriction resulting from the informational rights of another person or other law.</p><p>(4) Access must be available:</p><p>(A) at times and in a manner conforming to the express terms of the agreement; and</p><p>(B) to the extent not expressly stated in the agreement, at times and in a manner reasonable for the particular type of contract in light of the ordinary standards of the business, trade, or industry.</p><p>(b) In an access contract that gives the licensee a right of access at times substantially of its own choosing during agreed periods, an occasional failure to have access available during those times is not a breach of contract if it is:</p><p>(1) consistent with ordinary standards of the business, trade, or industry for the particular type of contract; or</p><p>(2) caused by:</p><p>(A) scheduled downtime;</p><p>(B) reasonable needs for maintenance;</p><p>(C) reasonable periods of failure of equipment, computer programs, or communications; or</p><p>(D) events reasonably beyond the licensor's control, and the licensor exercises such commercially reasonable efforts as the circumstances require.</p><p>2000, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0101'>101</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP0996'>996</a>.</p>
