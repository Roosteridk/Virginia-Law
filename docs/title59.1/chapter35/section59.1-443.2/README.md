# ยง 59.1-443.2 Restricted use of social security numbers.

<p>A. Except as otherwise specifically provided by law, a person shall not:</p><p>1. Intentionally communicate another individual's social security number to the general public;</p><p>2. Print an individual's social security number on any card required for the individual to access or receive products or services provided by the person;</p><p>3. Require an individual to use his social security number to access an Internet website, unless a password, unique personal identification number or other authentication device is also required to access the site; or</p><p>4. Send or cause to be sent or delivered any letter, envelope, or package that displays a social security number on the face of the mailing envelope or package, or from which a social security number is visible, whether on the outside or inside of the mailing envelope or package.</p><p>B. This section does not prohibit the collection, use, or release of a social security number as permitted by the laws of the Commonwealth or the United States, or the use of a social security number for internal verification or administrative purposes unless such use is prohibited by a state or federal statute, rule, or regulation.</p><p>C. In the case of any (i) health care provider as defined in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-581.1/'>8.01-581.1</a>, (ii) manager of a pharmacy benefit plan, (iii) insurer as defined in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/38.2-100/'>38.2-100</a>, (iv) corporation providing a health services plan, (v) health maintenance organization providing a health care plan for health care services, or (vi) contractor of any such person, the prohibition contained in subdivision 2 of subsection A shall become effective on January 1, 2006.</p><p>D. This section shall not apply to public bodies as defined in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/2.2-3701/'>2.2-3701</a>.</p><p>E. No person shall embed an encrypted or unencrypted social security number in or on a card or document, including, but not limited to, using a bar code, chip, magnetic strip, or other technology, in place of removing the social security number as required by this section.</p><p>2005, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?051+ful+CHAP0640'>640</a>; 2008, cc. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?081+ful+CHAP0562'>562</a>, <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?081+ful+CHAP0820'>820</a>.</p>
