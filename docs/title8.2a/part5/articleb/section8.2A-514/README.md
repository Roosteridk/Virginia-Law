<p>(1) In rejecting goods, a lessee's failure to state a particular defect that is ascertainable by reasonable inspection precludes the lessee from relying on the defect to justify rejection or to establish default:</p><p>(a) If, stated seasonably, the lessor or the supplier could have cured it (ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.2A-513/'>8.2A-513</a>); or</p><p>(b) Between merchants if the lessor or the supplier after rejection has made a request in writing for a full and final written statement of all defects on which the lessee proposes to rely.</p><p>(2) A lessee's failure to reserve rights when paying rent or other consideration against documents precludes recovery of the payment for defects apparent in the documents.</p><p>1991, c. 536; 2004, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?041+ful+CHAP0200'>200</a>.</p>
