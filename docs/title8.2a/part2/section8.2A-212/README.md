# ยง 8.2A-212 Implied warranty of merchantability.

<p>(1) Except in a finance lease, a warranty that the goods will be merchantable is implied in a lease contract if the lessor is a merchant with respect to goods of that kind.</p><p>(2) Goods to be merchantable must be at least such as;</p><p>(a) Pass without objection in the trade under the description in the lease agreement;</p><p>(b) In the case of fungible goods, are of fair average quality within the description;</p><p>(c) Are fit for the ordinary purposes for which goods of that type are used;</p><p>(d) Run, within the variation permitted by the lease agreement, of even kind, quality, and quantity within each unit and among all units involved;</p><p>(e) Are adequately contained, packaged, and labeled as the lease agreement may require; and</p><p>(f) Conform to any promises or affirmations of fact made on the container or label.</p><p>(3) Other implied warranties may arise from course of dealing or usage of trade.</p><p>1991, c. 536.</p>
