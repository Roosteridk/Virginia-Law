# § 57-19 Conveyances of land to benevolent and other associations to be subject to certain sections.

<p>When any conveyance of land has been or shall be made to trustees for the use of any society of Freemasons, Odd Fellows, Sons of Temperance, posts of Veterans of Foreign Wars or of the American Legion, Spanish War Veterans, Disabled American Veterans and of other associations of veterans of the armed forces of the United States, or any other benevolent or literary associations, or school league, or other groups organized for rural community civic purposes or improvement of farm life or operations of like purposes and not for profit, or if without the intervention of trustees such conveyance has been made since March 31, 1848, or shall be hereafter made for such use, §§ <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/57-8/'>57-8</a>, <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/57-11/'>57-11</a>, <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/57-14/'>57-14</a> and <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/57-15/'>57-15</a> shall be construed as if they were expressly made applicable to such association, post or society. Whenever such association, post or society has been in undisputed possession, for a period of twenty-five years or more, of any real estate, and for which there is no deed of record, § <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/57-17/'>57-17</a> shall be construed as if it was expressly made applicable to such association, post or society.</p><p>Code 1919, § 47; 1946, p. 230; 1954, c. 129; 1956, c. 203.</p>
