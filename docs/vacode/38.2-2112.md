# § 38.2-2112 Temporary insurance contracts; duration; what deemed to include.

A. Oral or written binders or other temporary insurance contracts may be made and used for a period not exceeding sixty days pending the issuance of the policy, and shall be deemed to include all agreements and provisions set out in §§ [38.2-2104](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/38.2-2104/) and [38.2-2105](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/38.2-2105/) and all applicable endorsements designated in the temporary insurance contract. Unless otherwise expressly provided, the contract shall be deemed to include the usual provisions, stipulations and agreements which are commonly used in this Commonwealth in effecting the insurance.

B. No temporary insurance contract shall include any provision or agreement which is inconsistent with or waives any provision, stipulation, agreement or condition required by § [38.2-2104](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/38.2-2104/) or § [38.2-2105](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/38.2-2105/). However, the cancellation provision and the provision fixing the hour of inception may be superseded by the express terms of the temporary insurance contract.

Code 1950, § 38-181; 1952, c. 317, § 38.1-371; 1986, c. 562.
