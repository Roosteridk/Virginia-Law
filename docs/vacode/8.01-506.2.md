# § 8.01-506.2 Proceedings in court of county or city where execution debtor resides.

When pursuant to subsection B of § [8.01-506](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-506/), a summons requires the execution debtor to appear before a court of the county or city in which the execution debtor resides, or of a county or city contiguous thereto, the execution creditor may have the case filed or docketed in that court as follows:

1. The execution creditor shall file with that court an abstract of the judgment rendered.

2. The execution creditor shall pay a fee to that court in accordance with the provisions of § [16.1-69.48:2](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/16.1-69.48:2/) or subdivision 17 of § [17.1-275](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/17.1-275/).

3. After docketing or filing the abstract of judgment and payment of any fees, the court shall issue the summons and any subsequent executions on the filed or docketed judgment, including a subpoena duces tecum pursuant to § [8.01-506.1](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-506.1/), and shall conduct such hearings and enter such orders pursuant to §§ [8.01-507](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-507/), [8.01-507.1](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-507.1/), [8.01-508](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-508/), [8.01-509](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-509/), and [8.01-510](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/8.01-510/) as may be required.

4. The execution creditor shall file in both courts any releases or satisfactions of judgment.
