# § 37.2-801 Admission procedures; forms.

A. Any person alleged to have a mental illness to a degree that warrants treatment in a facility may be admitted to a facility by compliance with one of the following admission procedures:

1. Voluntary admission;

2. Admission of incapacitated persons pursuant to § [37.2-805.1](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-805.1/); or

3. Involuntary admission by the procedure described in §§ [37.2-809](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-809/) through [37.2-820](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-820/).

B. The Office of the Executive Secretary of the Supreme Court of Virginia shall prepare the petitions, orders, and such other legal forms as may be required in procedures for custody, detention, and involuntary admission pursuant to Articles 4 (§ [37.2-808](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-808/) et seq.) and 5 (§ [37.2-814](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-814/) et seq.) of Chapter 8, and shall distribute such forms to the clerks of the general district courts and juvenile and domestic relations district courts of the Commonwealth. The Department shall prepare the preadmission screening report, examination, and such other clinical forms as may be required in proceedings for custody, detention, and admission pursuant to § [37.2-805](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-805/), and Articles 4 (§ [37.2-808](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-808/) et seq.) and 5 (§ [37.2-814](http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/37.2-814/) et seq.) of Chapter 8, and shall distribute such forms to community services boards, mental health care providers, and directors of state facilities.
