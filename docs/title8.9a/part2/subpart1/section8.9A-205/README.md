# ยง 8.9A-205 Use or disposition of collateral permissible.

<p>(a) When security interest not invalid or fraudulent. A security interest is not invalid or fraudulent against creditors solely because:</p><p>(1) the debtor has the right or ability to:</p><p>(A) use, commingle, or dispose of all or part of the collateral, including returned or repossessed goods;</p><p>(B) collect, compromise, enforce, or otherwise deal with collateral;</p><p>(C) accept the return of collateral or make repossessions; or</p><p>(D) use, commingle, or dispose of proceeds; or</p><p>(2) the secured party fails to require the debtor to account for proceeds or replace collateral.</p><p>(b) Requirements of possession not relaxed. This section does not relax the requirements of possession if attachment, perfection, or enforcement of a security interest depends upon possession of the collateral by the secured party.</p><p>1964, c. 219, ยง 8.9-205; 1973; c. 509; 2000, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?001+ful+CHAP1007'>1007</a>.</p>
