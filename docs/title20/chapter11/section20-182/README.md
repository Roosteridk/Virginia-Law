# § 20-182 Privilege against disclosure of collaborative law communication; admissibility; discovery.

<p>A. Subject to §§ <a href='/vacode/20-183/'>20-183</a> and <a href='/vacode/20-184/'>20-184</a>, a collaborative law communication is privileged under subsection B, is not subject to discovery, and is not admissible in evidence.</p><p>B. In a proceeding, the following privileges apply:</p><p>1. A party may refuse to disclose, and may prevent any other person from disclosing, a collaborative law communication.</p><p>2. A nonparty participant may refuse to disclose, and may prevent any other person from disclosing, a collaborative law communication of the nonparty participant.</p><p>C. Evidence or information that is otherwise admissible or subject to discovery does not become inadmissible or protected from discovery solely because of its disclosure or use in a collaborative law process.</p><p>2021, Sp. Sess. I, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?212+ful+CHAP0346'>346</a>.</p>
