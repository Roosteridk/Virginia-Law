# ยง 13.1-906 Effect of dissolution.

<p>A. A dissolved corporation continues its corporate existence but may not transact any business except that appropriate to wind up and liquidate its business and affairs, including:</p><p>1. Collecting its assets;</p><p>2. Disposing of its properties;</p><p>3. Discharging or making provision for discharging its liabilities;</p><p>4. Distributing its remaining property; and</p><p>5. Doing every other act necessary to wind up and liquidate its business and affairs.</p><p>B. Dissolution of a corporation does not:</p><p>1. Transfer title to the corporation's property;</p><p>2. Subject its directors to standards of conduct different from those prescribed in ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/13.1-870/'>13.1-870</a>;</p><p>3. Change quorum or voting requirements for its board of directors or members; change provisions for selection, resignation, or removal of its directors or officers; or change provisions for amending its bylaws;</p><p>4. Prevent commencement of a proceeding by or against the corporation in its corporate name;</p><p>5. Abate or suspend a proceeding pending by or against the corporation on the effective date of dissolution; or</p><p>6. Terminate the authority of the registered agent of the corporation.</p><p>1985, c. 522; 2007, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?071+ful+CHAP0925'>925</a>.</p>
