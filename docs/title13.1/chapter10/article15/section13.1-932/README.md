# ยง 13.1-932 Corporate records.

<p>A. A corporation shall keep as permanent records minutes of all meetings of its members and board of directors, a record of all actions taken by the members or board of directors without a meeting, and a record of all actions taken by a committee of the board of directors in place of the board of directors on behalf of the corporation.</p><p>B. A corporation shall maintain appropriate accounting records.</p><p>C. A corporation or its agent shall maintain a record of its members, in a form that permits preparation of a list of the names and addresses of all members, in alphabetical order by class, if any.</p><p>D. A corporation shall maintain its records in written form or in another form capable of conversion into written form within a reasonable time.</p><p>E. A corporation shall keep a copy of the following records:</p><p>1. Its articles or restated articles of incorporation, all amendments to them currently in effect, and any notices to members referred to in subdivision L 5 of ยง <a href='http://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/13.1-804/'>13.1-804</a> regarding facts on which a filed document is dependent;</p><p>2. Its bylaws or restated bylaws and all amendments to them currently in effect;</p><p>3. Resolutions adopted by its board of directors creating one or more classes of members, and fixing their relative rights, preferences, and limitations;</p><p>4. The minutes of all members' meetings, and records of all action taken by members without a meeting, for the past three years;</p><p>5. All written communications to members generally within the past three years;</p><p>6. A list of the names and business addresses of its current directors and officers; and</p><p>7.
