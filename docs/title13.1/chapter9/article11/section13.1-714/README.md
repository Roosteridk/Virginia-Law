# § 13.1-714 Amendment of bylaws by board of directors or shareholders.

<p>A. A corporation's shareholders may amend or repeal the corporation's bylaws.</p><p>B. A corporation's board of directors may amend or repeal the corporation's bylaws except to the extent that:</p><p>1. The articles of incorporation or § <a href='/vacode/13.1-715/'>13.1-715</a> reserves that power exclusively to the shareholders; or</p><p>2. Except as provided in subsection E of § <a href='/vacode/13.1-624/'>13.1-624</a>, the shareholders in amending, repealing, or adopting a bylaw expressly provide that the board of directors may not amend, repeal, or reinstate that bylaw.</p><p>C. A shareholder of the corporation does not have a vested property right resulting from any provision in the bylaws.</p><p>Code 1950, §§ 13-10, 13.1-24; 1956, c. 428; 1985, c. 522; 2005, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?051+ful+CHAP0765'>765</a>; 2010, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?101+ful+CHAP0782'>782</a>; 2019, c. <a href='http://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?191+ful+CHAP0734'>734</a>.</p>
